Chat Anonymiser
Designed to let you use external AI models without exposing sensitive information. Before your prompt leaves your trusted environment, a local model detects personal data — names, emails, phone numbers, addresses, and more — and replaces each with a labelled placeholder that is contextually understandable, so the external model can still reason about your request naturally. You decide what gets anonymised: review every detected item, remove anything you're comfortable sharing, and add your own. Only you choose what leaves your environment. When the response arrives, every placeholder is swapped back so you read the answer with your real data restored.
Tips for best results
› When asking the model to rewrite or summarise text, wrap it in quotation marks — this helps the model understand what to act on versus your instructions.
› When adding a custom anonymisation, use a descriptive label (e.g. this person's SSN, client company name) so the model can reason about it in context.
› Glance at the anonymised text before sending — the local model may occasionally miss uncommon formats. Use Add anonymisation for anything that slipped through.
› Remove false positives freely — if something was flagged that isn't actually sensitive, hit Remove. Unnecessary placeholders can make the model's response less natural.
